Why We Started

In 2018, Dr. Emily Rodriguez watched her father lose his leg to diabetes—a complication that could have been prevented with earlier detection. Despite being an endocrinologist herself, she couldn't save him from a condition that develops silently, without pain, until it's too late.

That experience sparked a question: What if we could give diabetic patients the same early warning system that we have for heart conditions? What if a simple, comfortable sock could save hundreds of thousands of limbs each year?

Together with biomedical engineer Michael Chen, Dr. Rodriguez set out to build that solution. Three years and 47 clinical studies later, SensoSock was born—a medical device that's already helping thousands of people keep their feet, their mobility, and their independence.
